    What is NairaStable?

    A decentralized, over-collateralized stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the Nigerian Naira, built on Arbitrum. Every nNGN is backed by real ETH locked in a transparent smart contract.

    The Big Picture

    NairaStable lets anyone with ETH create nNGN — a digital version of the Naira that lives on the Arbitrum blockchain. It works the same way MakerDAO lets you create DAI from ETH, but instead of pegging to the US Dollar, nNGN stays pegged to the Nigerian Naira.

    You deposit ETH into a vault. That ETH is your collateral. The protocol then lets you mint (create) nNGN against it — up to a safe limit. When you want your ETH back, you repay the nNGN and get your collateral returned. Simple.

    Why Does This Exist?

    The Naira is used by over 200 million people, but there has been no reliable, transparent, decentralized digital version of it on any major blockchain. Most stablecoins are pegged to the US Dollar, which creates exchange rate exposure for people whose costs are in Naira.

    NairaStable brings the Naira on-chain so that:

    • Nigerians can hold and transfer value in their own currency without exchange rate risk
    • Developers can build Naira-denominated DeFi products on Arbitrum
    • Fintechs and payment companies can accept NGN-denominated digital payments
    • Liquidators can earn profit by keeping the system healthy

    How the Peg Works

    nNGN is not just "issued" — it has real ETH behind it at all times. The protocol enforces that every ₦1 of nNGN debt is backed by at least ₦2 worth of ETH. If that ratio drops below ₦1.50, anyone can pay off the debt and claim the ETH at a 10% discount (liquidation). This economic incentive keeps the system solvent and the peg credible.

    💡
    nNGN is a standard ERC20 token

    Any application that accepts USDC or USDT can accept nNGN with zero changes — it follows the same ERC20 interface. See the Developer Integration and Fintech & Non-Web3 pages for how to integrate it.

    Core Concepts

    Everything you need to understand before using the protocol — explained without jargon.

    The Vault

    Your vault is your personal account inside the NairaStable smart contract. When you deposit ETH, it goes into your vault as collateral. When you mint nNGN, your vault records that as your debt. Each wallet address has one vault — there's no limit to how many different positions you can have as long as they fit in that one vault.

    Collateral

    Collateral is the ETH you lock up as security. The protocol holds it on your behalf. You can withdraw it whenever your debt-to-collateral ratio allows. Currently, ETH is the only accepted collateral. Multi-collateral support (wBTC, ARB, stETH, USDC) is on the roadmap.

    The Collateral Ratio

    The Collateral Ratio (CR) tells you how well-backed your nNGN debt is. It's calculated as: 

    Collateral Ratio = (Value of your ETH in NGN ÷ Your nNGN debt) × 100%

Example:
  ETH price:       ₦3,200,000 per ETH
  You deposited:   0.001 ETH  →  ₦3,200 collateral value
  Your nNGN debt:  ₦1,200 nNGN
  Your CR:         (3,200 ÷ 1,200) × 100 = 266%   ✅ Safe
    ZoneCollateral RatioHealth FactorWhat it means
    Safe ≥ 200% ≥ 1.33 You're in good shape. Optionally mint more.
    At Risk 150% – 200% 1.0 – 1.33 Danger zone. Add more ETH or repay some nNGN.
    Liquidatable < 150% < 1.0 Your vault can be liquidated by anyone.

    The Health Factor

    The Health Factor is a single number that makes it easy to tell at a glance how safe your vault is:

    • Health Factor ≥ 1.0 → Your vault is healthy and cannot be liquidated
    • Health Factor < 1.0 → Your vault can be liquidated immediately

    Mathematically, a Health Factor of 1.0 exactly equals a 150% collateral ratio — the liquidation threshold. The further above 1.0 you are, the more buffer you have if ETH price drops. 

    Health Factor = (Collateral Value × Liquidation Threshold%) ÷ Debt Value
Health Factor = (₦3,200 × 75%) ÷ ₦1,200 = 2,400 ÷ 1,200 = 2.0  ✅ Very safe

    Why 200% Minimum to Mint?

    ETH prices can drop 20–30% in a single day. If you could mint at 150% (right at the liquidation line), a small price drop would instantly trigger liquidation. Requiring 200% at mint time gives a 50 percentage-point buffer before your vault becomes unhealthy. This protects both you and the protocol's solvency.

    ⚠️
    Markets move fast

    Even at 200% CR, a large sudden drop in ETH price can put your vault at risk. We recommend maintaining at least 250–300% if you're not actively monitoring the price.

    Fees

    The protocol charges small fees to sustain itself:

    ActionFeeWhere it goes
    Mint nNGN0.5% of amount mintedProtocol treasury
    Repay (burn) nNGN0.3% of amount repaidProtocol treasury
    Liquidation bonus (to liquidator)10% of debt value in ETHLiquidator
    Liquidation protocol cut2% of seized ETHProtocol treasury

    Providing Liquidity

    Make nNGN accessible to everyone — not just vault creators. When liquidity exists on Uniswap, anyone can swap ETH, USDC, or ARB directly for nNGN with no vault required.

    Why Liquidity Pools Matter

    Minting nNGN through the CDP vault requires depositing ETH as collateral — a great model for sophisticated DeFi users, but a barrier for someone who just wants ₦5,000 of nNGN to pay a bill or send a remittance. Uniswap liquidity pools remove that barrier.

    When liquidity providers (LPs) deposit nNGN and ETH (or USDC) into a Uniswap pool, any user can instantly swap their existing tokens for nNGN — like a 24/7 on-chain bureau de change, with no intermediary and no minimum deposit.

    ℹ️
    Testnet notice

    NairaStable is currently on Arbitrum Sepolia. Uniswap V3 is also deployed on Arbitrum Sepolia, so you can create test pools today. On mainnet, Uniswap V3 and Camelot pools will be the primary way everyday users acquire nNGN without opening a vault.

    Swapping Tokens for nNGN

    If an nNGN liquidity pool already exists on Uniswap, anyone can swap directly into nNGN in a few clicks — no vault, no collateral, no protocol interaction needed.

    1. Open Uniswap on Arbitrum Sepolia
      Go to the Uniswap app and connect your wallet. Make sure your wallet is set to Arbitrum Sepolia. Click Swap in the top navigation.
    2. Select Your Tokens
      Set the You pay token to ETH, USDC, or ARB — whatever you hold. Set the You receive token to nNGN. If nNGN doesn't appear in the list, paste the contract address directly: 0xb4C0f815950E1AEC52EdAf9a80586EBFF2c42946
    3. Enter an Amount and Review
      Enter how much you want to spend or receive. Uniswap shows the rate and estimated price impact. A pool must exist for the swap to go through — if the app shows "Insufficient liquidity", a pool needs to be created first (see below).
    4. Confirm the Swap
      Review the details and click Swap. Confirm the transaction in your wallet. nNGN arrives in your wallet within seconds once the transaction confirms on Arbitrum.

    Creating an nNGN Liquidity Pool

    If no pool exists yet — or you want to earn trading fees by providing liquidity — you can create or join an nNGN pool on Uniswap V3. You'll need nNGN to do this, so mint some from your vault first (see the User Guide).

    💡
    You need nNGN before you can add it

    Mint nNGN from your vault (Vault → Mint tab in the DApp), then use that nNGN alongside ETH or USDC when you create the pool. Both sides of the pair are required upfront.

    1. Go to Pool → New Position on Uniswap
      Open the Uniswap app, click Pool in the top nav, then New Position. Confirm your wallet is on Arbitrum Sepolia.
    2. Select the Token Pair
      Choose your base token (ETH or USDC) and set the second token to nNGN by pasting: 0xb4C0f815950E1AEC52EdAf9a80586EBFF2c42946. The recommended pairs are nNGN / ETH and nNGN / USDC.
    3. Choose a Fee Tier
      0.3% is standard for most token pairs. 0.05% works better when nNGN is paired with a stablecoin like USDC — lower fees attract more volume on stable pairs.
    4. Set a Price Range
      Click Full Range for the simplest option — your liquidity covers all prices and requires no active management. For concentrated liquidity (more fees, more active management), set a range around the current ETH/nNGN rate.
    5. Deposit Amounts and Confirm
      Enter how much of each token to deposit. The ratio is set by the current price. Click Preview, then Add, and confirm the transaction(s) in your wallet. You'll receive a Uniswap V3 LP position NFT representing your share of the pool.

    Recommended Pair

    Because the protocol currently only accepts ETH as collateral, nNGN is minted from ETH — making nNGN / ETH the only natural pair right now. After minting, you have nNGN in your wallet and residual ETH — both sides of the pool come directly from the same workflow with no extra tokens needed.

    ℹ️
    Uniswap is permissionless

    You can technically create any pair on Uniswap — nNGN/USDC, nNGN/ARB, or anything else — as long as you hold both tokens. The table above reflects what makes sense within the current protocol. As multi-collateral support is added in Phase 2, more pairs become the natural choice.

    Earning LP Fees

    Every swap through a Uniswap pool pays a fee to liquidity providers — split proportionally among all LPs in the pool. When you add liquidity to an nNGN pool, you earn your share of every trade that executes within your price range. Fees accumulate automatically and can be collected at any time from the Pool page on Uniswap.

    Understanding Impermanent Loss

    As an LP, you hold a mix of both assets. If the relative price between them shifts significantly — say ETH doubles in USD terms — the pool rebalances, and you end up holding more of the cheaper asset. The difference vs simply holding is called impermanent loss. For nNGN pools, if the ₦1 peg holds and your price range stays active, the fees you earn typically outweigh this effect.

    ⚠️
    LP positions carry risk

    Providing liquidity is not the same as holding tokens. If ETH moves sharply relative to nNGN, your position composition shifts. Understand how Uniswap V3 concentrated liquidity works before committing large amounts.

    Removing Liquidity

    To exit your position, go to the Uniswap Pool page, select your nNGN position, click Remove Liquidity, and choose how much to withdraw. Your tokens are returned to your wallet immediately, including any uncollected fees.

    What Liquidity Enables at Mainnet

    Deep nNGN liquidity on Uniswap and Camelot will be the main route to nNGN for everyday users on mainnet — people paying for goods, receiving remittances, or holding Naira-denominated savings. It also strengthens the peg: arbitrageurs can instantly buy nNGN when it trades below ₦1 or sell when it trades above, keeping the market price locked to the peg.

    Liquidations

    How the protocol stays solvent — and how you can earn profit by helping keep it healthy.

    What is a Liquidation?

    When a vault's collateral ratio drops below 150% (Health Factor below 1.0), that vault is considered unhealthy. To protect nNGN's peg, the protocol allows anyone to pay off the vault's debt and claim the ETH collateral at a discount. This is called liquidation.

    Liquidation exists to ensure that every single nNGN in circulation is always backed by more than ₦1 of ETH. Without it, the stablecoin would lose its credibility.

    ℹ️
    Liquidation is not a punishment

    If your vault gets liquidated, you lose some ETH but your debt is also cleared. The vault owner keeps any remaining ETH collateral after the liquidator takes their share. It's better than losing everything — but it's still something to avoid.

    The Liquidation Process

    1. Find an Unhealthy Vault
      The Liquidations page in the DApp lists all vaults with Health Factor below 1.0, sorted most urgent first. Or you can build a bot that watches getHealthFactor(address) on-chain.
    2. Hold nNGN
      You need to hold the nNGN amount you want to pay off. You can buy or borrow nNGN to do this. The more debt you cover, the more ETH you receive.
    3. Call Liquidate
      Pass the vault owner's address and the amount of debt you want to cover. The contract burns your nNGN and sends you ETH in return.
    4. Receive ETH + Bonus
      You receive ETH worth the debt you covered, plus a 10% bonus. The protocol keeps a 2% fee. Your net profit is 8% of the debt value.

    Profit Calculation Example

    Scenario:
  ETH price:            ₦3,200,000 per ETH
  Vault debt:           ₦1,500 nNGN
  Vault collateral:     0.001 ETH  (₦3,200)
  Collateral Ratio:     3,200 ÷ 1,500 = 213%  — wait, still above 150%

  But if ETH price drops to ₦2,000,000:
  Vault collateral:     0.001 ETH  (₦2,000)
  Collateral Ratio:     2,000 ÷ 1,500 = 133%  ← LIQUIDATABLE!

You liquidate (cover full ₦1,500 debt):
  ETH equivalent of ₦1,500 = 1,500 ÷ 2,000,000 = 0.00075 ETH
  + 10% bonus             = 0.000075 ETH
  Total seized ETH        = 0.000825 ETH
  - 2% protocol fee       = 0.0000165 ETH
  You receive             = 0.0008085 ETH  (₦1,617 at current price)
  Your profit             = ₦117  (7.8% return on ₦1,500 deployed)

    Who Can Liquidate?

    Anyone — except the vault owner themselves. There is no whitelist, no permission needed, no registration. If you hold nNGN and a vault is unhealthy, you can liquidate it.

    Partial Liquidations

    You don't have to pay off all the debt. You can cover any portion of it. The vault remains open after a partial liquidation. If it's still below 150% CR, it can be liquidated again by anyone (including you, right away).

    Liquidators are economically incentivised to cover as much debt as possible in a single call because the 10% bonus applies to the full amount covered.

    For Vault Owners: How to Avoid Liquidation

    • Keep your Health Factor comfortably above 1.5 at all times
    • Deposit more ETH when price drops and your CR approaches 200%
    • Repay some nNGN debt to restore your ratio
    • Set price alerts for ETH drops — a 25% price drop at 200% CR leaves you at 150% exactly
    🚨
    You cannot self-liquidate

    The protocol explicitly prevents you from liquidating your own vault. If your position becomes unhealthy, you need to repay debt or deposit more collateral — not call liquidate yourself.

    TypeScript
    import { useReadContract } from 'wagmi';
import { NSEngineABI } from './abis/NSEngine';

const ENGINE = '0x1307b715C44d1DbC97e5A6657f34795d2D6cF854';

function useVault(address: `0x${string}`) {

    abi: NSEngineABI,
    functionName: 'getVault',
    args: [address],
    // Returns: { collateralWei: bigint, debtNgn: bigint }
  });

// Health factor (1e18 = 1.0)

  return useReadContract({

// Current ETH/NGN price — scaled to 1e18

    Depositing ETH (payable transaction):

    TypeScript
    import { useWriteContract } from 'wagmi';
import { parseEther } from 'viem';

function useDeposit() {
  const { writeContractAsync } = useWriteContract();

  return async (amountEth: string) => {
    return writeContractAsync({
      address: ENGINE,
      abi: NSEngineABI,
      functionName: 'depositCollateral',
      value: parseEther(amountEth),  // ETH sent as msg.value
    });
  };
}

    Minting nNGN:

    TypeScript
    import { parseUnits } from 'viem';

function useMintNgn() {

  return async (amountNgn: string) => {
    return writeContractAsync({
      address: ENGINE,
      abi: NSEngineABI,

    Burning nNGN (requires ERC20 approve first):

    TypeScript
    const NNGN = '0xb4C0f815950E1AEC52EdAf9a80586EBFF2c42946';

async function burnNgn(amount: bigint) {
  // Step 1: Approve ENGINE to spend nNGN
  await writeContractAsync({
    address: NNGN,
    abi: ERC20ABI,
    functionName: 'approve',
    args: [ENGINE, amount],
  });

  // Step 2: Burn (repay)
  await writeContractAsync({
    address: ENGINE,
    abi: NSEngineABI,
    functionName: 'burnNgn',
    args: [amount],
  });
}

    Fintech & Non-Web3 Integration

    You don't need to be a blockchain company to accept nNGN. If you can make HTTP requests and run Node.js, you can integrate nNGN into any existing platform.

    What You Actually Need

    Forget everything you think is complicated about Web3. To work with nNGN you need:

    • An RPC endpoint (a URL that talks to the blockchain) — free to get
    • The nNGN contract address
    • A standard ERC20 ABI (a JSON description of the token's functions)
    • ethers.js, web3.py, or any EVM library in your language of choice

    That's it. No special web3 infrastructure. No blockchain node to run yourself.

    Verify a Specific Payment Transaction

    JavaScript
    // Given a transaction hash from a customer
async function verifyPayment(txHash, expectedPayer, expectedAmount) {
  const receipt = await provider.getTransactionReceipt(txHash);
  if (!receipt || receipt.status !== 1) return false;  // Failed tx

  // Parse the Transfer event from the receipt
  const iface = new ethers.Interface(ERC20_ABI);
  for (const log of receipt.logs) {
    try {
      const parsed = iface.parseLog(log);
      if (parsed.name === 'Transfer'
          && parsed.args.from.toLowerCase() === expectedPayer.toLowerCase()
          && parsed.args.to.toLowerCase() === YOUR_ADDRESS.toLowerCase()
          && parsed.args.value >= ethers.parseUnits(expectedAmount, 18)) {
        return true;
      }
    } catch (_) {}
  }
  return false;
}

    What About Users Without Crypto Wallets?

    If your customers don't have wallets, you have two main options:

    • Custodial wallets: Create and manage wallets on behalf of your users. You store the private keys. When they want to send nNGN, you do it for them server-side. Simple to implement, but you hold responsibility for the funds.
    • Embedded wallets: Use a service like Privy, Dynamic, or Magic.link to create non-custodial wallets tied to a phone number or email. Your users get a wallet without needing to know anything about seed phrases.
    ℹ️
    On-chain settlement is final

    Unlike card payments that can be reversed, nNGN transfers are irreversible once confirmed on-chain. Design your payment flow accordingly — verify amounts before triggering transfers.

    Oracle Architecture

    How real-world NGN prices get on-chain — and the engineering decisions behind it.

    The Problem

    The protocol needs to answer one question at every transaction: "How many Naira is 1 ETH worth right now?"

    Smart contracts can't directly access the internet. They can only read data that's already been put on-chain. Services called oracles bridge this gap — they read off-chain prices and push them on-chain.

    Chainlink — the most trusted oracle in DeFi — has a live ETH/USD feed on Arbitrum Sepolia. But no oracle (not Chainlink, not Pyth) provides a USD/NGN feed on any EVM testnet. The Naira is not yet covered by decentralised oracle networks.

    The Solution: Two-Feed Composition

    We solve this by chaining two price feeds together: 

    ETH/NGN = ETH/USD  ×  USD/NGN

         Chainlink       Custom Oracle
         (live on        (keeper-driven,
          Arbitrum        fetches real forex
          Sepolia)        rate every 30 min)

    Both feeds implement the exact same interface — Chainlink's AggregatorV3Interface. When Chainlink or Pyth adds a native USD/NGN feed on mainnet, we simply swap one address for another. No contract code needs to change.

    The Custom USD/NGN Oracle

    We deployed USDNGNOracle.sol — a smart contract that:

    • Stores the latest USD/NGN exchange rate with 8 decimal precision (same as Chainlink)
    • Can only have its price updated by a designated keeper address
    • Implements AggregatorV3Interface so the engine treats it identically to Chainlink
    • Reverts all reads if the price hasn't been updated in more than 2 hours
    • Rejects any single update that moves the price more than 10% (manipulation guard)

    The Keeper Script

    A Node.js keeper process runs 24/7 on a server (Railway for always-on uptime). Every 30 minutes it:

    1. Calls the ExchangeRate-API to get the live USD/NGN rate
    2. Converts it to 8-decimal on-chain format (rate × 1e8)
    3. Calls setPrice() on the USDNGNOracle contract
    4. The on-chain price is now fresh — valid for the next 2 hours
    Keeper Script (simplified)
    async function pushPrice(oracle) {
  // 1. Fetch real USD/NGN rate from forex API
  const response = await fetch(`https://api.exchangerate-api.com/...`);
  const data = await response.json();
  const rate = data.conversion_rates.NGN;  // e.g. 1600 (NGN per $1)

  // 2. Convert to 8-decimal on-chain format
  const onChainPrice = BigInt(Math.round(rate * 1e8));  // e.g. 160000000000

  // 3. Push to oracle contract
  const tx = await oracle.setPrice(onChainPrice);
  await tx.wait();
  console.log(`Price updated: 1 USD = ${rate} NGN`);
}

// Run every 30 minutes
setInterval(() => pushPrice(oracle), 30 * 60 * 1000);

    How the Engine Uses Both Prices

    // Inside OracleLib.sol
function getEthNgnPrice(address ethUsdFeed, address usdNgnFeed)
  returns (uint256 ethNgnPrice)
{
  uint256 ethUsd = staleCheckLatestRoundData(ethUsdFeed);  // e.g. 2000e18 ($2,000)
  uint256 usdNgn = staleCheckLatestRoundData(usdNgnFeed);  // e.g. 1600e18 (₦1,600 per $1)

  // Compose: ETH/NGN = ETH/USD × USD/NGN
  ethNgnPrice = (ethUsd * usdNgn) / 1e18;  // e.g. 3,200,000e18 (₦3,200,000 per ETH)
}

    Staleness Protection

    The protocol has multiple layers protecting against stale prices:

    GuardWhat it catchesError thrown
    Heartbeat check Price older than 3 hours on either feed OracleStalePrice
    Round completion check Chainlink mid-update / incomplete round OracleStaleRound
    Zero-price guard Feed returned zero or negative price OracleInvalidPrice
    10% deviation guard Single keeper update that moves price >10% DeviationTooHigh

    If the keeper goes offline for 2+ hours, all vault operations except depositing more collateral will revert. This is intentional — it's safer to pause the protocol temporarily than to liquidate vaults based on a stale price.

    Security Trade-offs

    RiskMitigation
    Keeper key compromised 10% max deviation per update prevents sudden manipulation
    Keeper goes offline 2-hour staleness check halts the protocol gracefully
    Centralized USD/NGN feed Designed to be replaced by Chainlink/Pyth when available on mainnet
    Forex API goes down Railway auto-restarts the keeper; API has 99.9% uptime SLA
